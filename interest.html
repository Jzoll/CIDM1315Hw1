My Interests

Spanish

Because of my diverse background, I've always wanted to learn Spanish. This desire only intensified when I consistently found myself in want of the skill while working and when I realized that it was a skill I wanted to be able to pass down to my future children. Deciding to be more proactive about it, last fall I started taking online classes with Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México. This spring I completed level 3. This fall I've decided to take a break from formal Spanish classes and work on my current skills. Learning a new language has been both fun and challenging.

Yoga

I started taking classes on March 28th, 2022 and was immediately hooked. Since that initial class, I've attended +40 yoga classes. I'll admit that I had no idea how challenging yoga would be or that it would be such a fulfilling experience. By embracing yoga I have become stronger, more balanced, more mindful, and much better at managing my stress.

Hiking

There's nothing quite like the outdoors. Feeling the fresh air, appreciating the scenery, being able to disconnect: nature is a wonderful thing. When the weather works out I love going hiking with my husband. Most of our hiking has been in Texas but, just this year, we made it out to see the California Redwoods. It was incredible to see the grandness of the trees.

Reading

Sometime after getting my echocardiography registry I rediscovered my love of reading. Not just books but graphic novels as well. Currently, I'm reading “How to Do the Work” by Nicole LePera, “El Principito” by Antoine De Saint-Exupery, “How to Tell a Story” by The Moth, “Dubliners” by James Joyce, “Godzilla: World of Monsters”, and “Chainsaw man” by Tatsuki Fujimoto. I've been having a lot of fun by jumping between genres, it feels like a fun exercise for my brain. Unfortunately, by falling back into reading, I've also opened myself to accumulating a backlog of books to read, but the silver lining is that everything still manages to fit on my bookshelves.

Mycology

Last year I purchased my husband a mushroom grow kit. From that kit, we grew lion's mane mushrooms. Feeling nostalgic and in want of an apartment-friendly hobby, I decided to further explore the world of fungi. I bought more mushroom kits and grew shiitake, oyster, and reishi mushrooms. Growing mushrooms has been a really interesting experience and I feel like I've learned a lot and still have much to learn. The most gratifying part about mycology is watching the mushrooms grow and figuring out fun ways to cook them! My goal for the end of the year is to figure out how to make my own grow kits.


Trivia: The worlds largest fungus is 2,385 acres.
