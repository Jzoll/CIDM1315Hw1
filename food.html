Favorite Food

Balanced food, that gives you contrasting flavors, that keeps you going back for more is my favorite. Additionally, I enjoy food with fun textures. I love trying and cooking new foods. Above all else, I love cooking with my husband and seeing what we come up with. Having a multi-ethnic background, Iive been exposed to all sorts of foods from a young age. When I was in elementary I remember bringing in asian candies for my predominantly hispanic classmates to try. It seemed like everyone had a different reaction but what stood out to me was how important it is to try new things. By avoiding new things thereis so much that a person can miss out on not just with food but in life.

I've left off this list but these are some foods that simply make my mouth water in no particular order.

  • Japanese Sea Bream
  • Uchi in Austin has constructed this fantastic piece of sushi. It's complemented with a yuzu sauce and melts in your mouth.

  • Egg Custard Buns
  • Qi in Austin makes these wonderful dim sum and they are always fresh. They come piping hot and when you bite into the bun your mouth is met with a delicious egg filling that makes you think “Who knew an egg filling could be this tasty?”

  • Pan de Muerto
  • Once a year I eat this bread. It's chewy and tastes like orange. It's nostalgic for me and something that goes wonderful with a warm beverage.

  • Concha
  • I've had many conchas but never like the ones at La Panaderia here in San Antonio. The bread is wonderfully fluffy with just the right amount of sweetness. If you're lucky enough to eat one fresh you'll realize that it's what dreams are made of.

  • Braised and Charred Spanish Octopus
  • Bliss in San Antonio makes this dish. It's flavorful, moist, tender, yet crispy on the outside.

  • Takoyaki
  • In San Antonio, Hero's Ramen makes fabulous Takoyaki. It's covered in tasty toppings and filled with tender octopus meat.

  • Watermelon
  • I've always enjoyed mine with a sprinkling of salt or plum powder.

  • Tamarind
  • For me, this is a classic flavor that is bright, tangy, and sweet.

  • Mole and Hot Chocolate
  • When visiting Oaxaca, Mexico I ate these every day, and every day they were simply delicious. My only regret is that I didn't bring more of the powders and sauces back home.

  • My Mother-in-law's Tamales
  • She makes all types every year during the colder months. My favorites are the mole-flavored ones.
